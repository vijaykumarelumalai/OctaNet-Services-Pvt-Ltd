        <h3>1. Who are these courses for?</h3>
        <p>Our courses are designed for anyone interested in learning web development, regardless of their prior experience. Whether you're a complete beginner or have some familiarity with HTML and CSS, our courses are structured to accommodate learners of all levels.</p>
        
        <h3>2. Do I need any prior coding experience?</h3>
        <p>No prior coding experience is required to enroll in our beginner-friendly courses. We start from the basics and gradually progress to more advanced topics, ensuring that everyone can follow along and understand the material.</p>
        
        <h3>3. How long are the courses?</h3>
        <p>The duration of each course varies depending on the content and pace of learning. Our courses typically range from a few weeks to a couple of months, with new content released regularly. However, you can learn at your own pace and access the course materials at any time.</p>
        
        <h3>4. Are the courses self-paced?</h3>
        <p>Yes, our courses are self-paced, allowing you to study and complete assignments at your own convenience. You'll have access to the course materials for an extended period, allowing you to revisit lessons and review concepts as needed.</p>
        
        <h3>5. Do I receive a certificate upon completion?</h3>
        <p>Yes, upon successful completion of a course, you will receive a certificate of completion to showcase your achievement. Our certificates are recognized by industry professionals and can be a valuable addition to your resume or portfolio.</p>
        
        <h3>6. Can I interact with instructors and fellow students?</h3>
        <p>Absolutely! Our courses include discussion forums and live Q&A sessions where you can interact with instructors and fellow students.         <h3>7. What happens after I enroll?</h3>

        window.addEventListener('scroll', function () {
            const header = document.querySelector('.header');
            const scrollTrigger = document.querySelector('.scroll-trigger');
            const triggerHeight = scrollTrigger.getBoundingClientRect().top;

            if (triggerHeight <= 0) {
                header.classList.add('header-scrolled');
            } else {
                header.classList.remove('header-scrolled');
            }
        });

        function showTime() {
            document.getElementById('currentTime').innerHTML = new Date().toUTCString();
        }

            showTime();
